**The Fed and the front end are the switch now — geopolitics is largely priced.**
After two windows of de-escalation, the market sold the front of the curve on the
hawkish-Fed/inflation outlook *even as* US-Iran talks advanced and oil fell. Treat
rates, the Fed path, and inflation prints (PCE, September-hike odds) as the lead
variable, and read risk through duration and rotation rather than the next headline.
There are now two *demonstrated* challengers that can temporarily reclaim the lead:
a geopolitical reversal (a Strait-of-Hormuz disruption / US-Iran roadmap break), and —
newly shown the week of June 23–26 — an **AI-capex / valuation unwind** that drives the
equity tape (a global chip / mega-cap selloff) while the front end stays anchored. Both
are challengers to watch, not the standing switch: in the June 23–26 episode the rout
stayed an intra-equity rotation and rates held as the calm anchor, so the switch did not
change hands.
